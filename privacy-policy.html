Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

The short version: This app runs entirely in your browser. There is no server. Your email data is never sent to us or any third party. We store nothing. We track nothing.

What this app does

Inbox Sort and Tidy helps you sort and tidy your Gmail inbox by scanning your messages, grouping them by sender, and letting you archive or label them in bulk. It uses pattern matching to suggest categories (newsletters, receipts, notifications) -- no artificial intelligence, no language models.

What data is accessed

When you connect your Gmail account, the app requests permission to read and modify your email labels. Specifically, it accesses:

  • Message metadata only: sender name, sender email address, subject line, date, and the presence of the List-Unsubscribe header.
  • Email bodies are never accessed. The app requests metadata format only.
  • Labels: the app reads your existing labels and can create new ones (Newsletters, Receipts, FYI).

How data is used

Email metadata is used solely to display the sender frequency table in your browser and to execute the archive and label actions you explicitly approve. The data is processed entirely within your browser. It is never transmitted to any server operated by us or any third party.

Data storage

There is no server-side storage. This app has no backend, no database, and no server. Your email metadata exists in your browser's memory only during your active session. When you close the tab, the data is gone.

The only data persisted is your sender category preferences (e.g. "archive emails from this sender"), stored in your browser's localStorage. These preferences contain sender email addresses and the category you assigned -- no email content, no subject lines, no message counts. You can clear these at any time via the app or by clearing your browser data.

Data sharing

None. Data flows directly between your browser and Google's Gmail API servers. No third-party server ever receives your email data. The app loads one external script (Google's Identity Services library from accounts.google.com) which is required for authentication.

Authentication

The app uses Google's Identity Services to authenticate you. When you click "Connect Gmail", a Google popup asks you to sign in and grant permission. The access token returned by Google is stored in your browser's memory (not in cookies, not in localStorage, not on any server) and expires automatically after one hour. There are no refresh tokens -- you re-authenticate each time you use the app.

What the app cannot do

  • Cannot read email bodies (only metadata is requested)
  • Cannot delete emails (only archive and label operations are used)
  • Cannot send emails on your behalf
  • Cannot access your contacts, calendar, or any non-Gmail data
  • Cannot store your data on any server (no server exists)

Cookies and tracking

This app does not use cookies. It does not use analytics. It does not use tracking pixels, beacons, or any third-party measurement tools. There is no way for us to know who uses this app or how they use it.

Children's privacy

This app is intended for users aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly process data from children. Because the app stores no data, there is no children's data to manage or delete.

Security

All communication is encrypted via HTTPS. The app enforces a Content Security Policy that blocks unauthorised scripts, connections, and resources. The source code is open source and publicly auditable.

Your rights

You can:

  • Disconnect at any time using the "Disconnect" button in the app, which revokes the access token immediately.
  • Revoke access via your Google Account security settings at myaccount.google.com/permissions.
  • Clear saved preferences via the app's settings or by clearing your browser's localStorage.
  • Review the source code -- the app is open source.

Google API Services User Data Policy

This app's use of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be published at this URL. Material changes will be noted with the updated date at the top of this page.

Decommission

If this app is shut down, users will receive at least 30 days notice. Since no user data is stored server-side, decommission requires no data migration or deletion. Users should revoke the app's access via their Google Account settings (myaccount.google.com/permissions).
